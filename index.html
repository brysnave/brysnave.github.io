About Me

My name is Bryan Snavely. I will be obtaining my bachelor's degree in computer engineering in May from the University of Florida. I've lived in Gainesville the past two years, but I didn't take the typical route to earn my bachelor's degree.

While most kids I meet in my degree program came straight from high school, I didn't know exactly what I wanted to do when I graduated. So, instead of getting a business degree, like most of my friends did, I started working while going to a state college part-time. For a little while, I was actually working three jobs at once (one full-time and two part-time) on top of going to school part-time. After earning an associate's degree, I decided I wanted to study computer engineering.

The only problem was I didn't have any of the prerequisites I needed. I would have to start over from the beginning. I didn't let that worry me, though. I took a job as a server at a fine dining restaurant to make ends meet and began to dig into the material I needed to learn.

After two years of going to school part time while working 60-70 hours a week, and becoming part of the management team at the restaurant, I finally finished the required prerequisites. I applied to the University of Florida and was accepted!

After the long road I've taken to get my bachelor's degree I can honestly say that I have learned the value of hard work by working hard.

I work efficiently in a fast paced environment. I am very organized, a quick learner, I thrive when working with a team and accomplish tasks that are given to me in a professional and proficient manner.

I am proficient in HTML, CSS, Swift, Java, C++, VHDL, MIPS Assembly Language, XMEGA Assembly Language, MATLAB, Mean.js, Scrum and Agile development. I also have taken courses that have emphasized technical writing and professional communications.

My career interests are web application development, cyber security and transportation technology, but I am learning more every day and find that the more I know, the more I'm interested in. I would love to be a valued member of an innovative team that is making a difference in the world.
